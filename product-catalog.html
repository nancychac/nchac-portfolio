UITS PRODUCT CATALOG

UX Research • User Testing • Web Development

  • Timeline: March 2024 - July 2024
  • Team: Digital Experience (DX) Team
  • Role: UX Researcher, Web Developer
  • Project Type: Professional Work
  • Tools: Drupal

BACKGROUND

The UMass President’s Office University Information Technology Services (UITS) has a product catalog page on the organization's website. This is the hub for employees to find product information that is licensed within UMass. This includes a landing page including cards of the listed products and individual pages. The team modified the web pages to make it accessible and up-to-date for employees.

PROJECT PROCESS

  1. ORIGNAL STATE

    2. The product catalog has not been updated within a few years. It included products that are no longer licensed. There is also a group of new products that had to be added to the catalog. The individual article pages were also outdated and not accessible-friendly such as including broken URL links.

  2. CONTENT PLANNING & DESIGN

    3. The cards on the landing page were redesigned to be more accessible. The general format of the product pages was also redesigned. From top to bottom, it now includes the description and a login button to the product, Key Features, Eligibility, a Step-by-Step guide on How to Access the product, Related Knowledge Articles, and Resources from the Product site, and Related Products from the catalog. The team added preliminary content and reformatted the product catalog website in the Drupal staging environment. Each member had their own set of pages to update.

  3. INTERVIEW & TESTING

    4. I interviewed the Subject Matter Experts of the products to receive feedback on the new page designs. This retained their opinions on the new page format and auditing product information. For the user testing, three colleagues were selected based on their experience with the product catalog. There were tasked-based questions instructing the users to find the product, its page, and related knowledge articles. The screen size adjustment (zooming-in) was also included.

    Overall questions were asked about their user experience such as how they felt about the webpage layout, how often they use the product catalog, what they most like, and improvements for the site. Two users previously used the product catalog while one barely did before the test. Overall, they were able to complete the task-based questions, one important part is the search bar added to the landing page was the most helpful for all. There were also positive responses to the new layout and two users easily adapted to it. They critiqued specific products from UMass that should be further promoted on the product catalog page. There were also questions on specific wordings of section titles and labels which confused them.

  4. IMPLEMENTATION

    5. The team applied the feedback from the Subject Matter Experts and users to the staging page sites. After one final review, the team added these changes to the production site.

OUTCOME

The UITS Product Catalog is now up-to-date and accessible to more employees. The team will continue to update the website when new content is necessary.

REFLECTION

This was my first experience of being a user experience researcher. I learned the best practices of user testing such as the procedure to conduct it and what questions to ask. I also further understood my colleagues' different needs and wants for the product catalog. This experience made me consider the end-user’s perspective which can be easily overlooked during the design and development stages.

This was my introduction to web development. It was also my first time using Drupal. It provided a good opportunity to familiarize how UMass creates web content for the organization. I also learned the process of web development, from creating prototypes on the staging environment to going live in production.
