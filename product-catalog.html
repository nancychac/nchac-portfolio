UITS PRODUCT CATALOG

UI Design • Web Content & Development

  • Timeline: March 2024 - July 2024
  • Team: Digital Experience (DX) Team
  • Role: UX Researcher, Web Developer
  • Project Type: Professional Work
  • Tools: Lucidchart, Drupal

BACKGROUND

The UMass President’s Office University Information Technology Services (UITS) has a product catalog page on the organization's website. This is the hub for employees to find product information that are licensed within UMass. This includes a landing page including cards of the listed products and individual pages. The team modified the webpages to make it accessible and up to date for users.

PROJECT PROCESS

  1. ORIGNAL STATE

    2. The product catalog has not been updated within a few years. It included products that are no longer licensed. There are also a group of new products that need to be added to the catalog. The individual article pages were also outdated and not accessible-friendly such as including broken URL links.

  2. CONTENT PLANNING & DESIGN

    3. The cards on the landing page were redesigned to be more accessible. The general format of the product pages was also redesigned. From top to bottom, it now includes the description and a log in button to the product, Key Features, Eligibility, a Step-by-Step guide on How to Access the product, Related Knowledge Articles, Resources from the Product site, and Related Products from the catalog. The team added preliminary content and reformatted the product catalog website in the Drupal staging environment. Each member had their own set of pages to update.

  3. INTERVIEW & TESTING

    4. I interviewed with the Subject Matter Experts (SME) of the products to receive feedback on the new page designs. This retained to their opinions on the new page format and the content describing the product. For the user testing, three colleagues were selected based on their experience with the product catalog. There was tasked based questions instructing the users to find the product, its page, and related knowledge articles. The screen size adjustment (zoom-in) was also included.

    Overall questions were asked about their user experience such as how they felt about the webpage layout, how often they use the product catalog, what did they most like and what needs improvement on the site. Two of the users frequently use the product catalog while one barely uses it. Overall, they were able to complete the task based questions, one important part is the search bar added in the landing page was the most helpful for all. There was also positive responses on the new layout and two users easily adapted to it. They critiqued about specific products from UMass that should be pushed to the product catalog. There were also questions on specific wordings of section titles and labels which confused them.

  4. IMPLEMENTATION

    5. The team applied the feedback from the SME’s and users to the staging page sites. After another round of review, the team added these changes to production.

OUTCOME

The UITS Product Catalog is up to date and accessibility-friendly. The team will continue to update the website when necessary.

REFLECTION

This was my first experience of being a user experience researcher. I learned the best practices of user testing such as the procedure to conduct it and what questions to ask. Also further understanding the different needs and wants from Subject Matter Experts and users. This experience made me consider the end-user’s perspective which can be easily overlooked as a developer.

It was also my first time using Drupal. It provided a good opportunity to familiarize how UMass creates their websites using it. Also going over the process of creating websites in staging then publishing in production. This was my introduction to web development.
