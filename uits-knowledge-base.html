UITS KNOWLEDGE BASE

UI Design • Web Content & Development

  • Timeline: March 2024 - July 2024
  • Team: Digital Experience (DX) Team
  • Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Project Type: Professional Work
  • Tools: Salesforce Knowledge, Lucid, Drupal

BACKGROUND

This branched from another project involving updating the content of the University Information Technology Services (UITS) knowledge base articles in the UMass President’s Office site. The current knowledge base site only has a search engine for users to look at specific articles/ sources. My team is looking to refresh and organize the web page. The final website will be accessible and updated with relevant content.

PROJECT PROCESS

  1. ORIGNAL STATE

    2. The webpage includes a search bar and a list of published knowledge-base articles starting from the most recent. It is run by Coveo. There are no UI elements used besides the website header and the top menu options: Support Home Page, Knowledge Articles, View Your Cases, Create Case. The UMass President's Office knowledge base site is run by Salesforce.

  2. PEER RESEARCH

    3. My design references were knowledge base pages from other universities. The team also provided ideas from other organizations that utilized UI elements such as icons and containers on their Help and FAQ pages. From my references, I noted potential elements we can use from the examples to the page design.

  3. CONTENT PLANNING & DESIGN

    4. In our initial discussion about the knowledge articles, we received feedback from knowledge base article content owners about the webpage layout. They recommended adding the article creation date, related articles, responsible department/team on the article pages. For the homepage, the team agreed to have containers of general categories (i.e., “around the office”, onboarding, specific products) of articles in the knowledge base. Refer to the wireframe below for more details of my design process.

    We researched potential widgets and elements used on Salesforce CRM marketplace that could be used based on my wireframe design. This is to know what our options are for development. I also interviewed the article content owners to audit knowledge articles and discussed what content needs to be added to Salesforce Knowledge. There were also questions about updating the article periodically and sending automated reminders to them. Implementation is still pending and will be the next step.

WIREFRAME

  • This wireframe was created using Lucidchart from the Lucid Visual Collaboration Suite. The landing, category, and article pages are navigated from left to right. The design choices are based on interviewed user preferences and research references.
  • On the landing page, I kept the search bar and added a directory of categories most of the articles might relate to. These are organized as cards that users can click on to go to the category pages. The right sidebar includes cards of the most recent and popular articles. The third bottom card includes a list of article topic tags.
  • The category page includes groups of articles retaining frequently asked questions. On top of the section are buttons users can click to jump to a specific section. The right sidebar includes a search bar on the right and the same containers as the landing page. The difference is it includes a list relating to the specific category.
  • The article page continues the content. The details container includes the dates of when it was last modified and created. It also has a responsible team/department. I also added a list of related articles and links to sources outside of the knowledge base.

OUTCOME

This project is still ongoing and content planning is still underway. We’re working towards updating the web content on Salesforce.

REFLECTION

This project provided the opportunity to do user interface design as well as interview users for our knowledge base. So far, I understand that each user has different preferences for product features. From my previous project on the CRM & DX Site refresh, I applied my takeaways in UX design to the project. This includes practicing creating low-fidelity wireframes, graphic placements, and what would be most useful for users on specific pages.

Going into Salesforce Knowledge and updating the content will be the next step. I expect this to be a learning curve since this will be my first hands-on experience using Salesforce Knowledge for web development. However, this will be an excellent opportunity to expand my skill set.
