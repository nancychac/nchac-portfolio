KNOWLEDGE BASE SITE (UMPO, UITS)

UI Design • Web Content & Development

PROJECT INFO

  • Timeline: March 2024 - July 2024
  • Team: Digital Experience (DX) Team
  • Role: UX Designer
  • Project Type: Work
  • Tools: Lucidchart, Drupal, Salesforce

This came from another project involving refreshing the knowledge base articles on the UMass President's Office site. The current knowledge base site only has a search engine for users to look at specific articles/ sources. The team is looking to refresh and organize the web page.

PROCESS

ORIGINAL STATE

The webpage includes a search bar and a list of published knowledge-base articles starting from the most recent.

PEER RESEARCH

I used knowledge base pages from other universities and support/help centers from other organizations.

CONTENT PLANNING

We received feedback from knowledge base article owners. They recommended preferences of what should be included on the webpage. Also what article information should include such as creation date, related articles, responsible department/team.

IMPLEMENTATION

This will be the next step.

CONTENT & DESIGN

  • The landing, category, and article pages are connected. On the landing page, I kept the search bar and added a directory of categories most of the articles might relate to. These are organized as containers/ cards which users can click on to go to the category pages. The right sidebar includes containers of the most recent and popular articles. The third bottom container includes a list of article tags.
  • The category page includes groups of articles retaining to most frequently asked questions. On top of the section are buttons users can click to jump to a specific section. The right sidebar includes a search bar on the right side and the same containers as the landing page. The difference is it includes a list relating to the specific category.
  • The article page continues the content. The details container includes the dates of when it was last modified and created. It also has a responsible team/department. I also added a list of related articles and links to sources outside of the knowledge base.

CURRENT STATE

This project is still ongoing. The team is currently in the design and planning stage. I’m currently interviewing content owners in auditing these articles.
