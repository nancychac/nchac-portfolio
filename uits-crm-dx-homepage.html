UITS CRM & DX TEAM SITE

UX Design • Web content • Web Development

  • Timeline: March 2024 - July 2024
  • Team: Digital Experience (DX) Team
  • Role: UX Designer
  • Project Type: Work
  • Tools: Lucidchart, Drupal

BACKGROUND

The UITS CRM & DX team was recently merged into one team. The team doesn't have an official page site on the umassp site. The team would like to have a homepage that includes an intro to the team and all of the resources for support.

PROCESS

ORIGINAL STATE

The organization site included pages for the digital experience (DX) team. The CRM team is new and merged with DX. It only included a page summarizing the team's roles and lacked support and resources they can provide.

PEER RESEARCH

Used other university websites as references for creating the customer relationship management (CRM) page. I looked to see what elements we can incorporate into the site using Drupal.

CONTENT PLANNING

A content checklist and guideline document was created. The team intended the team site to be a hub for users to find information on web content, accessibility, CRM, automation, and support. The assigned webpage designers created their wireframes for the site. This helped the team brainstorm and curate what the team needs and recognize what can be implemented based on accessibility guidelines and Drupal.

IMPLEMENTATION

The team implemented the web content and design into the staging environment. Revisions were made to the guidelines as the team updated the pages in staging.

CONTENT & DESIGN

  • The content of the wireframe was from the guideline document. I considered what the team could do using Drupal and tried to push the limitations of other designs we could utilize.
  • I added graphics and icons to lists to further stylize the page and visualize the listed items for users.
  • Support Page: The team decided to have a potential right sidebar, including a button linked to create cases for the IT help desk and DX & CRM support information. I created two options for the teams on the Support page with or without the right sidebar.

CURRENT STATE

The elements from the home and support page are implemented to production. The team is currently working on creating the product page. This became a separate project as it involves further research on users' needs for learning and using products the organization licensed for employees.

SITE PAGE

TAKEAWAYS

  • I learned that there are limitations to design when working with certain programs and applications such as Drupal. However, always aim to test those limitations.
  • Accessibility is vital to user experience. Also coming up with the most optimal design solutions that will make development easier.
  • This project helped me set the foundations of being a UX/UI designer and web developer.
